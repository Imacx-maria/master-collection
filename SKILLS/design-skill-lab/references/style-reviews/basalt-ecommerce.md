# Basalt E-Commerce Review Checks

Load during Phase 4 when the chosen style is Basalt E-Commerce.

## Style-Specific Questions

1. **Is EB Garamond used Regular only?** Never bold. Italic reserved for accent-word emphasis (first word of headlines). Bold serif breaks the refined feel.
2. **Do products float on gray cards with shadows?** Signature treatment — product imagery on #E4E4E4 surface with soft drop shadow. Plain white product cards are wrong.
3. **Is the palette strictly grayscale?** Any color creeping in (even subtle) breaks Basalt. Accent colors are forbidden — the product photography carries all color.
4. **Are layouts split 50/50 with internal asymmetry?** Split image+copy layouts are the structural move. Centered everything reads as default e-commerce template.
5. **Is section padding generous (96px+)?** Luxury breathing room is the point. Tight sections feel budget.
6. **Does headline italic land on the first word only?** "*Radiant* skin" not "Radiant *skin*". The italic-first-word is a signature pattern.
7. **Are corners sharp on cards and buttons?** 0–4px max. Rounded e-commerce cards read as generic Shopify.
8. **Is Inter body copy + Garamond display — not the reverse?** Sans body + serif display is the pairing. Serif body would feel slow, sans display would feel generic.

## Common Regressions

- Accent colors added for "conversion psychology" — wrong style for that
- EB Garamond Bold instead of Regular Italic for emphasis
- Product cards without the gray floating treatment
- Tight section spacing to "fit more above the fold" — breaks the luxury brief
- Rounded buttons/cards from imported component systems
