# Creative Studio Review Checks

Load during Phase 4 when the chosen style is Creative Studio.

## Style-Specific Questions

1. **Does the hero use the dark charcoal band (#2D2F2E)?** The dark/light alternation is the structural rhythm. Starting on light breaks the gallery feel.
2. **Is portfolio imagery shown in full color, large, unapologetic?** Desaturated, tinted, or shrunk imagery kills the style. Work should dominate.
3. **Is the coral gradient scale (#FFF0EB → #FFC8B8 → #FF9D80 → #FF531F) used for process/timeline sections?** The 4-step gradient is a signature move. Don't flatten it to a single coral block.
4. **Is coral (#FF531F) used as accent, not surface?** CTA fills and one statement process band — not page backgrounds or large hero blocks.
5. **Do dark sections use muted light text (#AFB6B4 / #767F7A) for secondary?** Pure white secondary on dark reads as harsh. The muted cools are intentional.
6. **Are cards rounded generously (16px+)?** Warmth against the dark frame comes from curves. Sharp cards break the premium agency feel.
7. **Does the typography scale aggressively?** Heroes at 96px+, not 48px. Agency sites need dramatic type.
8. **Are dark and light bands clearly alternated, not randomly mixed?** Dark → light → dark → coral → dark is a rhythm. Randomly scattered dark sections feel arbitrary.

## Common Regressions

- Coral accent becoming the dominant color instead of the hit
- Desaturated portfolio imagery to "match the brand" — wrong instinct
- Inter at weight 400 for headlines — needs 700 for agency punch
- Sharp card corners creeping in from default libraries
- Process sections as a single block instead of the 4-step gradient
